Umngqusho recipe

Description

Umngqusho, also known as “samp and beans,” is a South African favourite dish which is packed with flavour and sure to satisfy.

When preparing Umngqusho, you cannot be in a hurry as you need the samp and beans to simmer slowly to ensure a soft, creamy result. No matter how tempting, avoid stirring! so you don't end up with a dish that is not appetising at all.

Ingredients

  • 500g samp, well rinsed
  • 800g dried sugar beans, well rinsed
  • water to cover
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • ½ cup leeks, chopped
  • 2 cubes beef stock
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Place the samp and beans together in a pot with water.
  2. Cover and bring it to a boil and then reduce the heat and cook until soft for at least 2 hours. Do not stir or it will become excessively starchy.
  3. Keep checking to ensure that the mixture does not stick at the bottom of the pot. Add water if necessary.
  4. After about an hour, add the onion, leek and stock. Allow simmering until it is soft.
  5. Add the butter and seasoning and stir with a wooden spoon. It must not be dry.
  6. Serve with umleqwa or lamb curry.
